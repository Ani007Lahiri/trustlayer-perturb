1 · The idea, in one page (unchanged core, sharpened claims)
The gap we exploit
The field can now predict a gene knockdown’s effect in a T cell — but deep models barely beat a linear
baseline on accuracy, so competing there is a dead game, and “recovering” a lab’s own known hits is what
every other team does. The empty, winnable lane is decision under uncertainty: a model that reports its own
confidence, abstains when it should, and tells a lab where to spend a real experiment. Think AlphaFold’s
pLDDT — the confidence layer is what made the predictions usable. Nobody has built that for perturbation
biology.
THE ONE LINE TO LEAD WITH “Every team here can predict which gene to fix. None can tell you when
that prediction is wrong — so no lab bets a real experiment on it. We built the CD4⁺ T-cell model that
reports its own confidence, proves it with Type 1 diabetes genetics held out of both training and its own
scoring axis, kills its own weak pick through an enforced veto, and hands a Gladstone lab Monday’s
CRISPRi experiment — every number reproducible from pinned public data.”
The four-beat arc
1. Predict — a cheap, borrowed base predictor of knockdown effect (we do not compete here).
2. Trust — a calibrated confidence score (not a “guarantee”) that beats a mean-collapse null and abstains on
predictions it can’t stand behind.
3. Recommend — turn trusted predictions into one genetics-anchored, direction-checked CRISPRi
experiment.
4. Self-critique — a critic that independently re-derives the numbers, audits the blinded gold set for leakage,
and has a real veto that physically blocks a bad nomination.
TWO NARRATIVE PRE-EMPTS — PUT THESE IN THE FIRST SLIDE/PARAGRAPH (A) Disambiguate
“calibration.” 2025 made metric calibration the hot topic (Arc VCC; Shift Bioscience). Say it explicitly:
“Not metric calibration — per-prediction coverage / trust. Different object.” Turn the collision into a wedge.
(B) Own the accuracy debate. Our premise (“DL doesn’t beat baselines,” Ahlmann-Eltze Nat Methods
2025) is contested by Miller et al. (Shift/Xaira, bioRxiv 2025). Cite both sides in the first paragraph —
acknowledging the debate is the sophistication move, and our trust layer is valuable whoever wins the
accuracy argument.

4 · Method rigor (survive the ML red-team)
Recovery metric — make it non-trivial and properly powered
R1
The problem. Recovering FOXP3/CTLA4/STAT1 is trivial (any Treg-aware ranker surfaces them by
construction), and n=9 positives in ~4,100 genes makes precision@k move in ~11-point steps with no
resolution — the single most demo-critical number is both easy to dismiss and statistically fragile.
The fix. (1) Score recovery on the non-canonical subset — MEOX1, LGALS3BP, CD1E, CD247
(secondarily CD5, IL10RA) — the genes a naive Treg ranker would not surface; recovering these is the
impressive result. (2) Report a threshold-free statistic over all 9 at once: AUPRC + median rank, tested
against a ≥10,000-iteration permutation null drawn from an immune/Treg-matched background (not
uniform genome-wide, which inflates significance). (3) Bake the obvious Treg genes into the null so
“surfacing FOXP3” counts toward the null, not toward success. Fix gold-set size to 9 everywhere. Present
as suggestive, not confirmatory.
Say “calibrated trust,” not “guarantee”
R2
Conformal prediction guarantees coverage on a scalar nonconformity score, not on a high-dimensional
gene-delta vector; and weighted-CP for “unseen genes” is support shift, not covariate shift, so the
likelihood ratio is undefined and the finite-sample guarantee does not hold where it’s most needed. ⇒
Commit to one scalar trust score per perturbation and state coverage on that object; estimate shift
weights in frozen ESM-2 space and report effective sample size as an honest “the guarantee broke
here” signal.
Beat a real null on the selective-risk curve
R3
Random abstention is a straw man — a mean-collapsed model still beats it. Put the mean/constant
baseline as a competing curve on the same plot (plus an “abstain-on-large-predicted-effect” heuristic).
No dominance over the mean baseline = no result. Score selective DEG-recall, not just selective MAE
(MAE is easy to win by predicting near-zero). ~½ day.
Make the OOD signal non-circular
R4
Compute kNN-OOD distance in frozen ESM-2 space, not the model’s own learned embedding (which is
optimized on the same data — circular). Add a 2-D trust signal (mean-distance × ensemble-
disagreement) to catch correlated mean-collapse that ensemble variance alone misses, since ensemble
members share the mean-collapse inductive bias.
5 · Claude use & demo — differentiate from the platform defaults
The problem. Claude Science ships a built-in actor-critic reviewer and per-figure reproducibility (code +
environment + message history) by default. To the judges — who built the workbench — “we have a
critic” and “we have a reproduce button” score ~0 on originality because they get both free. Multi-agent
also costs ~15× tokens and is non-deterministic run-to-run, which threatens a live reproduce-hash.
The fix — lead with what THIS critic uniquely does:
1. A hard, output-changing veto via tool-scoping (no-Write) — and show the leaderboard actually
change when it fires. A veto that alters the result is the differentiator; a veto that just comments is the
default.
2. Independent quantitative re-derivation + blinded-gold leakage audit — the critic recomputes effect
sizes itself and checks “did we ‘discover’ a gene because it was in a prompt?” This is the capability
the platform does not give free.
3. Cut the Elo tournament unless you can defend it with one worked ranking-flip; replace with a
transparent cross-modality convergence score over deterministic evidence — same result, fewer
moving parts.
4. Honest hallucination-delta: pre-register a countable checklist (citation-fidelity / effect-direction /
leakage) over a fixed N≈20–30 items on your own API key; in the demo show 3 verbatim caught
errors, not a slick aggregate %.
5. Remove the unverifiable “last three winners built adversarial self-verification” claim.
6. Reproduce-hash: rehearse on the demo machine with pinned BLAS threads and zero model calls;
hash the rounded scientific result (leaderboard ordering + recovery number), not raw floats.
